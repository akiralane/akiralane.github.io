Reinforcement learning: multi-armed bandits

Under supervised learning, we would instruct the correct answer/label.

With reinforcement learning, we evaluate the action undertaken.

The goal is to determine a sequence of actions - a “policy”.

Each action has a reward - and there’s an ongoing accumulation of data based on prior actions.

The key is balancing this accumulation of data and the immediate reward of picking apparently good options.

Actions in this context are transitions between states.

Uncertainty reduction

The non-greedy choice has an uncertain reward - but it could be better than the greedy choice!

We need more reward values to get a better estimate. Hence, we want to take some non-greedy choices. Can’t take two actions at once, though...

Action value method

Either exploit current knowledge to maximise immediate reward, or explore non-greedy actions with some sort of decision-making factor ε\varepsilonε﻿. We’ll come back to ε\varepsilonε﻿ in a bit.

Our estimate is the sum of the rewards for actions aaa﻿ over the number of times an action has been taken.

That is, it’s all the rewards for the action added together divided by the number of times we’ve taken the action.

Notation: I[a]=1\mathbb{I}[a]=1I[a]=1﻿ if aaa﻿ is true, 000﻿ otherwise.

This means that greedy action is biased by early choices, since we don’t have precise information. That means it’s often good to explore in the beginning.

To do this, we choose a parameter ε\varepsilonε﻿ for exploration.
