Multi-tape Turing Machines

Turing machines with multiple tapes can actually be simulated by single-tape TMs - their expressive power is precisely the same.

We usually focus on three-tape TMs, but the construction is easily generalised.

First, consider what a three-tape TM looks like!

We have three semi-infinite tapes and three independent read/write heads.

The transition function in a three-tape TM takes triples instead of symbols:

At each step, each head acts on their part of the triple:

  • Head 1 reads a, writes b and moves left
  • Head 2 reads b, writes b and moves right
  • Head 3 reads b, writes a and moves left

Step-by-step

Each step of the multitape machine is simulated in several steps in the single-tape machine.

Effectively,

  • Scan the top symbols in the tape until we find the marked symbol: that is, we look for the position of the top head.
  • Carry out the computation according to what the simulated head is looking at using the transition function.
  • Then, move all the way left.
  • We then repeat this process with the next row of symbols - scan until we find the marked symbol for this row, do the calculation it wants, scan back to the start.
  • This continues until every row has been simulated, at which point we start from the top again.

As you can see, this works with any number of tapes. We have to just keep adding rows.
