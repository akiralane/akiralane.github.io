The Pumping Lemma

Limitations of regular langauges

We've seen that the class of regular languages is quite robust:

It's the class of languages accepted by a family (DFA, NFA, ε\varepsilonε﻿NFA) of finite automata.

Then, are all languages regular..?

No: we can use a counting argument to prove this.

There are countably many regular expressions over a finite alphabet but uncountably many languages over the same alphabet.

That is, there are countably infinite strings in an alphabet, but the languages are comprised of sets of these strings. The powerset of a countably infinite set is uncountably infinite (∣S∣<<∣P(S)∣)|S| << |\mathcal{P}(S)|)∣S∣<<∣P(S)∣)﻿ - hence, not all languages are described by a regular expression and therefore not all languages are regular.
