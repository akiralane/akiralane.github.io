Why would you want to compromise an embedded device?

  • Influence of processes
    • Sabotage? Spoof sensor data?
  • Denial of availability
  • Leakage of authorisation
    • Access keys inside the firmware
  • Leakage of sensor information
  • Leakage of intellectual property
  • Device as a further attack vector
    • Hardware is often used in encryption, for example, so gaining access to the hardware could assist a decryption effort.

Attack Methods

Passive attacks are where you monitor normal operation of hardware (like IO, power consumption, etc) to infer implementation details.

This can help you find things like when encryption occurs - for example, a power spike could be one indicator of such an event.

These sorts of attacks are known as side-channel attacks.

Active attacks are where you manipulate the hardware into abnormal states (including destruction).

This could be used to gain insight into its design, or to compromise some aspect of it.

Reverse engineering typically uses active attacks.
