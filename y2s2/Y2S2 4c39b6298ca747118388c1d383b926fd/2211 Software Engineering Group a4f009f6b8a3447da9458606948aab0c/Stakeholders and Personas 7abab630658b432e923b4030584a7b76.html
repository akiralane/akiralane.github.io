Stakeholder Analysis

Who’s involved with the system?

Primary stakeholders: those who actually use and interact with the system.

For the ECS handin system, this would be students and lecturers.

Secondary stakeholders: those who don’t directly interact with the system, but provide input or receive output from it.

For the same example, lecturers can also be considered secondary stakeholders.

Tertiary stakeholders: those who don’t interact with the system but are affected by it.

This would be people like University management.

Facilitating: those who design, develop and maintain the system.

iSolutions, third-party developers.

Shared understanding - personas and scenarios

A persona describes an end user in some detail.

This detail includes things like background, job function and situation in the system.

Scenarios are descriptions of how a person interacts with the system and other personas.

They are independent of any technology - they may represent either current practice or improve practice.

In the envisioning stage, they typically reflect the domain “as-is” - current practice.

Technology Show-and-Tell helps all members of the design team to be familiar with current tech used in the domain and new tech which could create new opportunities.
