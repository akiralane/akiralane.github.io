Recall the evil coffee machine.

But sometimes, it could go to z2z_2z2​﻿ or z3z_3z3​﻿...

This could be due to some external result of concurrency - a race condition in the implementation, perhaps, or an environmental effect.

We’d like to be able to distinguish x0x_0x0​﻿ and z0z_0z0​﻿ at a finer scale than simulation.

Bisimulations

A bisimulation exists when two LTSs can match each other action-for-action.

Wait, but isn’t that the same as simulation equivalence?

It’s slightly different (although afaik in deterministic LTSs they are the same notion).

Look at the following LTSs:

They are simulation equivalent (aaa﻿ simulates eee﻿ and eee﻿ simulates aaa﻿). Nonetheless, they are not bisimilar.

Suppose that A moves from aaa﻿ to bbb﻿: B can match this by moving from eee﻿ to fff﻿.

But if A now moves to ddd﻿, there is nothing that B can do from that position.

Effectively simulation equivalence asks for there to exist some path such that the LTSs can match their moves.

Bisimulation, on the other hand, requires that they must match their moves for all paths.

Properties

Unions of bisimulations are bisimulations, so we also have the largest bisimulation  ∼~\sim ∼﻿ (called bisimilarity).

Bisimilarity is the finest “reasonable” equivalence. That is, if we are observing just the behaviour and not the direct state space, two bisimilar LTSs will look identical.
